# Academic Presentation Style Notes

Adapted from the "A Note on Style in Academic Presentations" section of the source PDF.

## Presentation Mode Differences

- Spoken academic communication is usually more personal and more explicitly signposted than written prose.
- First-person pronouns are acceptable and often useful in presentations.
- Signposting should help the audience follow the structure in real time.

## Operational Rules

- Reduce lexical density slightly relative to manuscript prose.
- Use direct audience-guidance phrases such as "I will first...", "The next part...", and "Let me now turn to...".
- Keep definitions and transitions explicit.
- Preserve technical accuracy while simplifying processing load for listeners.
