# SEGRESS Core Notes

Derived from the user-provided local PDFs `SEGRESS.pdf` and `SEGRESS22supplement.pdf`.

## What SEGRESS is for

SEGRESS is a software-engineering-specific reporting layer for secondary studies. It was designed to address recurring reporting weaknesses in software-engineering systematic reviews and to adapt PRISMA-style expectations to software-engineering evidence syntheses.

## Core claims extracted from the source

- Reports of software-engineering secondary studies are of variable quality.
- Review reports often make it difficult to locate essential information such as recommendations, quality-assessment logic, or validity-related details.
- PRISMA 2020 is useful but not sufficient by itself for all software-engineering secondary-study types.
- PRISMA item definitions can be extended to support mapping studies and qualitative reviews in software engineering.

## Operational use in this repository

Use SEGRESS when the task involves:

- reporting a software-engineering SLR
- reporting a software-engineering SMS
- reporting a qualitative secondary study
- adapting PRISMA-style reporting to software engineering
- improving completeness, traceability, and reproducibility of review reporting

## Reporting expectations to enforce

- state clearly why the secondary study was needed
- make the review type explicit
- report the search process transparently
- explain screening and eligibility criteria precisely
- report study-quality or risk-of-bias handling clearly
- distinguish evidence synthesis from mere study listing
- include explicit threats-to-validity or limitations treatment
- make the flow from evidence to conclusions auditable

## Scope cautions

- Use SEGRESS as a reporting and transparency layer, not as the sole method-design layer.
- For protocol design and review-process structure, combine SEGRESS with Kitchenham.
- For checklist-style reporting completeness, combine SEGRESS with PRISMA 2020.
