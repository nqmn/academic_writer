# Humanities Style Overlay

## Priorities

- Support interpretive nuance, conceptual precision, and sustained argumentative development.
- Allow more discursive transitions where they clarify reasoning.
- Preserve authorial voice while maintaining formal scholarly tone.

## Writing Behaviour

- Develop claims through close reading, comparison, and contextual framing.
- Use cautious evaluative language when interpreting texts, ideas, or historical positions.
- Permit longer sentences when their structure remains controlled.

## Preferred Moves

- Situate the argument within an intellectual conversation.
- Define key terms and interpretive boundaries explicitly.
- Show how the present reading revises, complicates, or extends prior interpretations.

## Avoid

- Mechanical empirical phrasing where interpretive language is more appropriate.
- Overly technical positivist framing for non-empirical claims.
- Flat summary that does not advance an argument.
