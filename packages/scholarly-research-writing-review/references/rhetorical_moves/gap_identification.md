# Gap Identification

## Acceptable Gap Types

- Empirical gap: the phenomenon has not been studied in a relevant setting or population.
- Methodological gap: previous work uses designs that limit inference, reproducibility, or generalisability.
- Theoretical gap: key concepts or mechanisms remain under-specified.
- Comparative gap: existing studies do not compare the cases, models, or conditions required for the current question.

## Writing Rules

- Anchor the gap in literature, not intuition.
- Avoid saying "no study exists" unless the claim is verifiable.
- Pair the gap with why it matters for knowledge, practice, or method.

## Strong Formulation Pattern

1. Summarise what the literature has established.
2. State what remains unresolved.
3. Explain the consequence of that unresolved point.
