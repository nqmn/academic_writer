# Theory and Theory Development: Guidelines for Establishing Theoretical Gaps, Foundations, Contributions, and Implications

**Citation:** Lim, W. M. (2026). Theory and theory development: Guidelines for establishing theoretical gaps, foundations, contributions, and implications. *Journal of Business Research, 202*, 115745. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbusres.2025.115745

**Keywords:** Theory, concept theory, relationship theory, typology theory, framework theory, integrated theory, theoretical gap, theoretical contribution, theoretical novelty, theoretical interestingness

---

## Core Argument

Theory guides knowledge acquisition and advancement. Existing guides on theory development suffer from three gaps: (1) fragmentation across multiple disconnected sources, (2) insufficient clarity on what constitutes theoretical interestingness, and (3) subjective thresholds for novelty. This guide offers a sequential, integrated framework addressing theoretical gaps, foundations, contributions, and implications.

---

## What Is Theory?

A theory is **a lens to comprehend, explain, and predict phenomenon**. It involves:
- **Abstraction** (what, where, when, who): defines context and scope
- **Assumption** (why, how): provides rationale for mechanisms and relationships

### Principles of a Good Theory

A good theory must be both:
1. **Conceptualizable** (explainable): clearly defined concepts and linkable relationships
2. **Operationalizable** (testable): measurable constructs and manipulatable variables

And must exhibit:
- **Generalizability** — applicable across different contexts
- **Extendibility** — can evolve and incorporate new discoveries
- **Falsifiability** — predictions can be empirically tested and disproven

### Key Distinction: Concept vs. Construct vs. Variable

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Concept** | Abstract idea that names/defines a phenomenon (not yet operationalized) |
| **Construct** | Concept made measurable for empirical use |
| **Variable** | Construct that varies across observations to enable analysis |

## Typology of Theory

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Concept theory** | Theory of a single concept | Customer brand engagement, Health equity |
| **Relationship theory** | Theory of direct relationships between concepts | Herzberg's dual-factor theory, Catering theory of dividends |
| **Typology theory** | Theory classifying multiple concepts into categories | VALS framework, Rogers' diffusion of innovations |
| **Framework (model) theory** | Theory addressing multiple relationships (direct, mediation, moderation) | Theory of planned behavior, Health belief model |
| **Integrated (hybrid) theory** | Combines multiple frameworks | UTAUT2, Integrated model of sustainable consumption |

## Theoretical Gaps: Types, Spotting, and Problematizing

### Three Types of Theoretical Gaps (3Us)
- **Unexplored** — aspects with no research attention (emerging topics, unexplored contexts)
- **Underdeveloped** — recognized but insufficiently developed theoretically or empirically
- **Uncertain** — conflicting or inconsistent findings in existing research

### Locating Gaps
- Systematic literature reviews (broad unexplored/underdeveloped/uncertain areas)
- Future research directions in recent publications (specific, narrow)
- Special issues in journals (emerging trends)

### Problematizing Gaps (Three Forms)
- **Debates** — contested assumptions creating confusion (necessity) or opportunity (importance)
- **Disconnects** — gaps between theory and practice highlighting relevance
- **Direness** — urgency when debates and disconnects are repeatedly ignored

> A gap without a problem has no meaning. A problem without a gap has no novelty.

## Theoretical Foundations: Five Key Questions

| Question | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| What is (are) the focal theory(ies)? | Identify primary theories informing the study |
| Why is (are) the focal theory(ies) important? | Justify relevance and credibility |
| How have existing studies used the focal theory(ies)? | Map prior applications and gaps |
| How is the present study using the focal theory(ies)? | Articulate specific application |
| How is the present study contributing back to the focal theory(ies)? | State what is affirmed, extended, refined, challenged, or refuted |

**Countercheck using the IMPACT Framework** (Hollebeek et al., 2025):
- **I**nterestingness, **M**atching, **P**arsimony, **A**pplicability, **C**onceptual rigor, **T**estability

## Theoretical Contributions: Types and Novelty

### Setting the Scene
- **Practical gaps**: necessity, importance, relevance, urgency
- **Theoretical gaps**: critique (strengths/weaknesses) + change (adapt, modify, extend, newly develop)

### Typology of Theoretical Contributions (ascending novelty)

| Type | Description | Novelty Level |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Theoretical generalizability** | Testing existing theory in new context/sample (reproducibility/replicability) | Lowest |
| **Theoretical adaptation** | Fitting existing theory to new context without significant core changes; adds contextualization | Low |
| **Theoretical modification** | Changing aspects/components to better fit a context; adds theoretical depth | Adequate |
| **Theoretical extension** | Creating an extension; adds theoretical breadth | Adequate |
| **New theoretical development** | Developing an entirely new theory | Highest |

### Strength of Theory Development

Three pillars:
1. **Theoretical support** — logical and evidence-based arguments underpinning assumptions
2. **Theoretical legitimacy** — empirical validation (theoretical soundness + granularity)
3. **Theoretical interestingness** — three forms:
   - **Counterintuitivity**: something unusual but sensible
   - **Paradoxicality**: co-existence of opposing ends
   - **Noteworthiness**: broader scope or deeper complexity than existing assumptions

## Theoretical Implications: The 3Rs

Theoretical implications establish the "so what" of theoretical contributions.

**Step 1 — Recap**
- Type of theory established
- Elements the theory explains/predicts (what, where, when, who, why, how)
- Type of contribution, level of novelty, and type of interestingness

**Step 2 — Retrospect**
- Revisit theoretical gaps identified at the outset
- Explain how contributions affirm, extend, refine, challenge, or refute existing understanding

**Step 3 — Reimagine**
- Identify new areas of inquiry
- Propose modifications or extensions
- Suggest new contexts or integrations
- Accompany with signposts of problematization and interestingness (the "why" of suggestions)

## Overall Framework Summary

```
Theory → Theory Development (iterative)
  Theoretical Gaps:
    Gap spotting (unexplored, underdeveloped, uncertain)
    Gap problematizing (debates, disconnects, direness)
  Theoretical Foundations:
    What, Why, How used, How applied, How contributed back
  Theoretical Contributions:
    Generalizability → Adaptation → Modification → Extension → New development
    Strength: support + legitimacy + interestingness
  Theoretical Implications:
    Recap → Retrospect → Reimagine
```
