# Gap Validation Methodology

## Purpose

Use this module whenever the agent identifies, writes, critiques, or defends a research gap.

Its function is to prevent fake novelty, exaggerated absence claims, and unsupported contribution framing.

## Core Rule

A research gap must be demonstrated from literature or evidence, not invented from convenience.

Do not write:

- `few studies have...`
- `no studies have...`
- `the literature lacks...`

unless the available evidence justifies the statement or the wording is appropriately softened.

## Gap Types

Distinguish among:

### Underexplored gap

The area has limited coverage, sparse cases, or thin evidence.

### Inconsistency gap

Studies exist, but findings conflict or remain unstable.

### Methodological gap

The topic has been studied, but design, data, or inference limitations remain.

### Theoretical gap

The mechanism, construct, or conceptual framing remains underdeveloped.

### Context gap

Findings may not transfer to an important setting, population, region, or application context.

### Integration gap

Relevant strands exist, but they have not been connected in a way that answers the present question.

## Validation Questions

Before stating a gap, check:

1. What does the literature already establish?
2. What exactly remains unresolved?
3. Is the issue absence, inconsistency, weakness, or transferability?
4. Why does the unresolved point matter?
5. Does the present study actually address that gap?

If the final answer is no, do not claim the gap.

## Safe Wording Rules

Prefer calibrated formulations such as:

- `the evidence remains mixed`
- `the mechanism remains unclear`
- `comparatively little attention has been given to...`
- `existing studies have largely focused on...`
- `the transferability of these findings remains uncertain`

Avoid absolute absence claims unless verified.

## Novelty Control

Do not equate:

- a different setting
- a small extension
- a repackaged method

with strong novelty by default.

Novelty should be framed according to the actual contribution:

- theoretical
- methodological
- empirical
- contextual
- integrative

## Gap-to-Contribution Link

A valid gap statement should connect directly to:

- the study aim
- the design choice
- the contribution claim

If the gap does not justify the contribution, the framing is weak.

## Review Pass

When a gap statement appears, review it for:

- evidence basis
- precision
- proportion
- actual relevance to the study

## Router Rule

Load this module whenever the task involves:

- identifying a research gap
- writing an introduction
- positioning a contribution
- literature synthesis
- novelty evaluation
