\## MOVE 1 — ESTABLISHING TERRITORY



\### Establishing Importance



Use patterns such as:



\* X is an increasingly important area in...

\* X has attracted considerable critical attention.

\* X is a major area of interest within...

\* Understanding the complexity of X is vitally important if...

\* Recent years have witnessed growing interest in...

\* X has become a central issue for...



\### Time-Framed Importance



Use:



\* Recent developments in X have led to...

\* Over the past decade...

\* In recent years...

\* The past thirty years have seen...

\* During the last decade...



\### Societal Importance



Use:



\* X plays a crucial role in...

\* X is fundamental to...

\* X is a major contributor to...

\* X is important for a wide range of...

\* Evidence suggests that X is among the most important factors...



\---



\## MOVE 2 — IDENTIFYING A NICHE



\### Problem Identification



Use:



\* X is a major problem in...

\* One of the greatest challenges...

\* There is increasing concern that...

\* X is increasingly recognised as...

\* The main challenge faced by researchers is...



\### Literature Gaps



Use:



\* Few studies have investigated...

\* There is little published research on...

\* The role of X remains largely unexamined.

\* To date, no large-scale studies have...

\* Surprisingly, X has not been closely examined.



\### Weaknesses in Previous Research



Use:



\* Previous studies have failed to...

\* Most studies have only focused on...

\* Existing literature lacks clarity regarding...

\* Most studies suffer from methodological limitations.

\* The generalisability of previous work is problematic.



\### Contradictory Evidence



Use:



\* Evidence for X has been mixed.

\* There is conflicting evidence concerning...

\* Studies have produced equivocal results.

\* Findings remain inconsistent and contradictory.



\### Knowledge Gaps



Use:



\* What remains unclear is...

\* Little is known about...

\* The mechanisms underpinning X are not fully understood.

\* Research has not yet determined...

\* The extent to which X influences Y remains unclear.

\* Academic Phrasebank is a content-neutral phraseological resource rather than a source of disciplinary facts.

\* section-based organisation is primarily a retrieval aid; many phrase sets are reusable across multiple sections.

\* explanatory notes on style, presentations, spelling, sentence structure, paragraph structure, and connecting words should be operationalised as separate modules.



The completed modular implementation therefore includes additional phrase libraries for:



\* classifying and listing

\* giving examples as support

\* indicating shared knowledge or understanding

\* writing about the past



It also includes operational note modules for:



\* academic style

\* presentation style

\* British and US spelling consistency

\* sentence structure

\* paragraph structure

\* connecting words



For direct agent use, the operational loading order is:



1\. `AGENTS.md`

2\. `runtime/system_prompt.md`

3\. relevant `styles/*.md`

4\. relevant `phrases/*.yml`

5\. relevant `anti_ai/*.md`

6\. relevant `disciplines/*.md`

7\. specialist reviewer-response or presentation modules where required



The machine-readable module index is stored in `runtime/agent_manifest.yml`.
