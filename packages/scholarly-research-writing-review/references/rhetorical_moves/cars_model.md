# CARS Model

## Move 1: Establishing Territory

- Explain why the topic matters within the field, society, or practice.
- Use present tense for established relevance and present perfect for recent scholarly attention.
- Open with importance, prevalence, impact, or unresolved complexity.

## Move 2: Identifying a Niche

- Expose a gap, contradiction, methodological weakness, or under-examined context.
- Make the niche specific enough to justify the study.
- Prefer defensible limitations over dramatic claims of absence.

## Move 3: Occupying the Niche

- State the aim, research question, or hypothesis.
- Indicate the research design if it helps clarify the contribution.
- Conclude with significance, scope, and optional structure overview.
