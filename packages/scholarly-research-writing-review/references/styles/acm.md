# ACM Style Overlay

## Priorities

- Make the computational problem, contribution, and evaluation design explicit.
- Preserve reproducibility and implementation clarity.
- Keep prose efficient while allowing sufficient method detail.

## Writing Behaviour

- Define the research artefact early, whether system, model, dataset, or framework.
- Tie claims to experimental setup, baselines, or analytical criteria.
- Use section openings that indicate what the reader will learn next.

## Preferred Moves

- Clarify assumptions, constraints, and tradeoffs.
- Compare against prior systems or studies in functionally precise terms.
- Use evidence-led discussion rather than promotional framing.

## Avoid

- Vague references to improvement without baselines.
- Under-specified evaluation claims.
- Excessive conceptual framing detached from implementation details.
