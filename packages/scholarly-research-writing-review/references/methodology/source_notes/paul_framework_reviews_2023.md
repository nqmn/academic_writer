# Paul, Khatri, and Kaur Duggal 2023 Notes

Derived from the user-provided local PDF `Paul, Khatri, Kaur Duggal - 2023 - Frameworks for developing impactful systematic literature reviews and theory building What, Why and H.pdf`.

## What this source adds

This source extends the methodology layer beyond procedural review design. Its main value is helping the agent design framework-based reviews that are more theory-building, conceptually integrated, and impactful.

## Core claims extracted from the local source

- systematic literature reviews are important for both synthesising extant knowledge and supporting theory building
- framework-based reviews are especially useful for producing impactful and robust synthesis
- a well-done review is structured carefully rather than assembled as a loose summary
- organising frameworks help integrate varied literature streams, identify gaps, and direct future research

## Frameworks highlighted in the source

- `TCCM`: theories, contexts, characteristics, methods
- `ADO`: antecedents, decisions, outcomes
- `5W+H`: who, when, where, what, why, how
- `IMO`: inputs, mediators, outcomes

## When to use this source

Use this source when the user asks for:

- a framework-based literature review
- theory building from a review
- a more impactful or conceptual review structure
- a review framework recommendation
- stronger future-research agenda construction

## Agent behavior derived from this source

- do not stop at protocol compliance; decide whether the review also needs an organising framework
- when theory building matters, recommend a framework explicitly and justify why it fits
- use the framework to structure extraction, synthesis, gap identification, and future research directions
- avoid paper-by-paper summaries when a framework-based synthesis is more appropriate

## Practical framework-selection guidance

- use `TCCM` when the user needs broad field mapping with strong future-research structuring
- use `ADO` when the literature can be synthesised around drivers, decisions or mechanisms, and outcomes
- use `5W+H` when the user needs a broad organising scaffold for descriptive and exploratory synthesis
- use `IMO` when the review logic centers on inputs, mechanisms or mediators, and resulting outcomes

## Scope caution

This source complements Kitchenham, PRISMA, and SEGRESS. It should not replace them for review-process transparency or reporting discipline. Use it to strengthen conceptual synthesis and theory-building value.
