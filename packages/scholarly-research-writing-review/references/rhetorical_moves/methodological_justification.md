# Methodological Justification

## Core Principle

- Explain not only what was done, but why that design best addresses the research question.

## Justification Targets

- Why the design fits the phenomenon
- Why the sample or corpus is appropriate
- Why the measures or instruments are suitable
- Why the analysis supports the intended inference

## Useful Pattern

1. Name the method.
2. State its analytical advantage.
3. Link that advantage to the study aim.

## Example Frame

"This approach was selected because it enables the study to capture both aggregate patterns and context-specific variation."
