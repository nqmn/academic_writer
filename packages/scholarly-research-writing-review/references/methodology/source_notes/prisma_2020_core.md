# PRISMA 2020 Core Notes

Derived from the user-provided local PDF `Prisma 2020.pdf`.

## What PRISMA 2020 is for

PRISMA 2020 is an updated reporting guideline for systematic reviews. Its central purpose is to help authors report why the review was done, what was done, and what was found in a transparent and complete way.

## Core reporting elements extracted from the source

- a 27-item checklist
- an expanded checklist with item-level reporting guidance
- an abstract checklist
- revised flow diagrams for original and updated reviews

## Agent interpretation

Use PRISMA 2020 to enforce:

- transparent review rationale
- transparent identification and selection reporting
- explicit description of synthesis methods
- auditable presentation of included studies and findings
- structured abstract reporting for review articles

## What PRISMA does not replace

- It does not replace the need for a domain-appropriate review method.
- It does not independently determine whether the review should be an SLR, SMS, or tertiary study.
- It does not replace software-engineering-specific reporting extensions such as SEGRESS.
