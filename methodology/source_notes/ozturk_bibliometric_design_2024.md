## What this source adds

This source strengthens the bibliometric layer by moving beyond analysis mechanics into full research-design logic. Its main value is the explicit fit between research aims, research questions, dataset construction, analysis selection, and reporting transparency.

## Four-stage design structure extracted from the local source

1. define the aim of the research
2. collect data on the relevant literature
3. perform analysis and visualization
4. interpret the findings and results

## Research-design expectations to derive from this source

- state the research aim and research questions explicitly in the introduction
- justify why bibliometric analysis is the right method for the stated aim
- ensure strong fit between research questions and the chosen data and techniques
- make the data-collection process transparent, systematic, and replicable
- explain every major filtering and sample-construction step
- avoid presenting a technically generated map without evaluation or substantive interpretation

## Database guidance extracted from the source

- database selection should consider field coverage, accessibility, export compatibility, filtering capabilities, and record-export limits
- `Web of Science` and `Scopus` are often the most practical bibliometric databases
- `Google Scholar` has broad coverage but weaker export suitability for many bibliometric workflows
- using multiple databases is not automatically better and can create overlap, harmonization, and duplication problems

## Search and filtering guidance extracted from the source

- bibliometric searches should usually be topic-based unless the purpose is specifically author- or journal-based
- search terms must align tightly with the research questions
- small changes in Boolean logic or truncation can change the corpus substantially
- inclusion and exclusion criteria should be justified transparently
- title and abstract screening is still needed to remove out-of-scope records
- the final sample should be downloadable in a format compatible with the chosen software

## Agent behavior to derive from this source

- do not suggest analyses that the available data cannot support
- do not treat transparency as optional; the dataset-construction path must be auditable
- when the user asks for a bibliometric paper structure, include aim, data source logic, filtering logic, analysis design, visualization logic, and interpretation
- when the user asks for research questions, make them technique-compatible

## Scope caution

- bibliometric studies can become weak when they are only labeled as bibliometric but functionally behave like narrative overviews
- software output alone does not constitute bibliometric research quality
